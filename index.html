How it works

When you wear our products, your face can still be recognized, but it is drowned in a sea of others created by the pattern, making it much harder to identify the real one. This false positive scenario prevents correct identification.

False Faces

False Faces To protect your biometric data, we developed knitwear with a detailed black and white pattern, which facial recognition systems identify as faces. This creates multiple “hits” and makes the correct identification of the wearer much harder.

They make mistakes

A study conducted at Südkreuz Berlin showed, that the margin of error in correctly identifying people is way too high. 70% of the participants were either identified falsely or as multiple different persons. When used in criminal investigations this can lead to innocent people being charged. There is a reason why biometric data are subject to the European General Data Protection Regulation.
